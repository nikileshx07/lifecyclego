The Lifecycle of a Butterfly

Egg: The female butterfly lays eggs on plant leaves.
Larva (Caterpillar): The egg hatches into a caterpillar, which eats leaves and grows rapidly, shedding its skin several times.
Pupa (Chrysalis): The caterpillar forms a protective shell, called a chrysalis, where it undergoes metamorphosis.
Adult (Butterfly): After metamorphosis, the butterfly emerges from the chrysalis, dries its wings, and takes flight to reproduce.
