The Lifecycle of Fungi

The life cycle of fungi typically involves a haploid phase and a diploid phase.
Haploid spores germinate into hyphae, which form a mycelium.
When two compatible mycelia meet, they fuse, leading to the formation of a diploid zygote.
This zygote undergoes meiosis to produce haploid spores, completing the sexual cycle.
Fungi can also reproduce asexually through various methods like budding, fragmentation, or the production of asexual spores.
