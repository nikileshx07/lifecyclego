The Lifecycle of Crow

A crow's life cycle begins with the laying of several eggs in a sturdy nest.
After incubation, young crows, called fledglings, hatch.
They are cared for by their parents, learning to fly and forage.
Once they are independent, they join a flock and establish their own territories.
As adults, they form pairs, build nests, and raise their own young, continuing the cycle.
