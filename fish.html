The Lifecycle of a Fish

Egg: Female fish lay eggs in water, often in nests or on surfaces, depending on the species. Eggs are fertilized by male fish, either externally or internally.
Larva: After hatching, the fish is a larva (often called a fry), which may have a yolk sac to provide nutrients. Larvae begin to swim and search for food once the yolk sac is absorbed.
Juvenile: As the fish grows, it becomes a juvenile, resembling an adult but smaller. Juveniles continue to develop and grow through feeding.
Adult: The fish reaches maturity and becomes capable of reproduction. Adults lay eggs to continue the cycle.
