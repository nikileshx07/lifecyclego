The Lifecycle of Bacteria

Bacteria, tiny organisms, reproduce rapidly through binary fission.
A single cell divides into two, then four, and so on, exponentially increasing in number.
Under ideal conditions, they can form colonies within hours. However, unfavorable conditions can halt their growth or lead to their death.
This rapid reproduction and adaptability make bacteria both beneficial and harmful to various ecosystems and human life.
