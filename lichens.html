The Lifecycle of Lichens

The life cycle of a lichen begins when a fungal spore lands on an alga or cyanobacterium. The fungal hyphae grow around and into the algal cells, forming a symbiotic relationship. This partnership results in a lichen thallus, a unique structure composed of both fungal and algal components. Lichens reproduce asexually through fragmentation or sexually via the production of fungal spores. The spores disperse and germinate, initiating the cycle anew.
