The Lifecycle of Deer

Fawn Stage:Birth: Deer give birth to one or more fawns, usually in the spring. Camouflage: Fawns are born with spotted coats to camouflage them from predators. Mother's Care: The mother deer nurses and protects her fawns, teaching them essential survival skills.
Juvenile Stage:Growth: Fawns grow rapidly, shedding their spotted coats as they mature. Socialization: They begin to interact with other deer, learning social behaviors and establishing hierarchies. Food Source: They transition from milk to a diet of plants and grasses.
Adulthood:Maturity: Deer reach sexual maturity around 1.5 to 2 years old. Rutting Season: During the fall, male deer (bucks) compete for dominance and mating rights. Reproduction: Successful bucks mate with female deer (does), leading to the birth of the next generation of fawns.
Aging:Senescence: As deer age, they may experience declining health and reduced fertility. Vulnerability: Older deer are more susceptible to predation and disease.
