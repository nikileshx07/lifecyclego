The Lifecycle of Dove

A dove's life cycle begins with the laying of two eggs in a simple nest.
After incubation, tiny, featherless squabs hatch.
These young doves are fed a special "pigeon milk" produced by their parents.
Once they are strong enough, they leave the nest and begin to explore their surroundings.
As adults, they form pairs and start their own breeding cycle, continuing the generation of new doves.
