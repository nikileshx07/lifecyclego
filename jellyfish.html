The Life of Jelly Fish

1. Adult Medusa:This is the stage we typically think of as a jellyfish. Adult medusae reproduce sexually by releasing eggs and sperm into the water. Fertilization occurs externally, and the fertilized eggs develop into...
2. Planula Larva:The fertilized egg develops into a tiny, free-swimming larva called a planula. Planulae are covered in cilia, tiny hair-like structures that help them swim. They eventually settle onto a hard surface, such as a rock or the seafloor.
3. Polyp:The planula larva attaches to the surface and transforms into a polyp. Polyps are small, cylindrical organisms that resemble miniature sea anemones. They reproduce asexually by budding, producing clones of themselves.
4. Ephyra:Some polyps undergo a process called strobilation, where they release tiny, immature medusae called ephyrae. Ephyrae are essentially baby jellyfish.
5. Juvenile Medusa:The ephyrae grow and develop into juvenile medusae.
6. Adult Medusa:The juvenile medusae mature into adult medusae, completing the cycle.
