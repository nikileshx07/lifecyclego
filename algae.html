The Lifecycle of Algae

Algae reproduce both sexually and asexually.
Asexual reproduction occurs through cell division or spore formation.
Sexual reproduction involves the fusion of gametes to form a zygote, which develops into a new organism.
The life cycle of algae can vary greatly, with some species having a simple life cycle dominated by a haploid phase, while others exhibit an alternation of generations with both haploid and diploid phases.
The specific life cycle depends on the algal group and species.
